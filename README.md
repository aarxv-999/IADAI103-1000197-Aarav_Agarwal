## Project Overview
**MindPatch** is a user-centered, AI-assisted digital wellness planner designed for students and young adults who experience digital fatigue, excessive screen time, and emotional overload in a hyperconnected environment.  
The app promotes mindful technology use by encouraging emotional self-awareness, healthy digital habits, and gentle detox routines through a calm, supportive interface.

This project was developed as part of the **Critical and Creative Thinking (CRS) â€“ Artificial Intelligence** summative assessment, focusing on human-centered design, ethical AI simulation, and usability-driven iteration.

## Problem Statement
Students today spend a significant portion of their day on digital devices, often leading to screen fatigue, stress, and reduced emotional well-being. Existing solutions either feel overwhelming, overly technical, or lack personalization. There is a need for a simple, emotionally supportive digital tool that helps users reflect on their mood, monitor screen usage, and receive personalized detox suggestions without causing guilt or pressure.

## Core Features (Compulsory Features Implemented)

1. **Mood Logging**  
   Emoji-based mood logging with optional notes to support emotional awareness.

2. **Screen-Time Input Dashboard**  
   Manual screen-time input with simple visual indicators.

3. **AI-Generated Detox Planner**  
   Personalized detox suggestions based on mood and screen usage.

4. **Gratitude Journal**  
   A calm journaling space for daily positive reflection.

5. **Habit Tracker with Rewards**  
   Habit streaks, badges, and progress tracking to build healthy routines.

## AI Logic (Simulated â€“ No Real AI Model)

The AI functionality in MindPatch is simulated using rule-based logic to demonstrate ethical and explainable AI behavior.

### Inputs
- User mood score
- Daily screen time
- General time-of-use context

### Logic Rules (Example)
- If mood is low and screen time is high â†’ suggest a longer digital detox break  
- If mood is neutral and screen time is moderate â†’ suggest short mindful breaks  
- If mood is positive â†’ reinforce habits and encourage gratitude journaling
